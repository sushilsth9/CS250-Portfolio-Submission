##How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
I start by considering the user's identity and the issue they wish to resolve in order to comprehend their needs. I make an effort to think like the customer rather than just a developer. I discovered that users do not care how the code works during the SNHU Travel project. They are concerned about the ease and problem-free booking of hotels and flights. I review user feedback, ask questions, and watch how users engage with the system to learn about their needs.
I was able to translate those needs into specific development tasks with the aid of user stories. A user story describes the user, their goals, and the significance of those goals. For instance, a traveler wants to look up flights and make an error-free reservation. When the feature is complete and functioning properly is explained by the acceptance criteria. Because everyone on the team was working toward the same objective, user stories helped to clarify the requirements and avoid misunderstandings. I was able to make the connection between what we build in the program and what the user expects.

##How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I think about who will use the program and what they want to do with it to figure out what they want. I don't just think like a programmer; I also try to think like a customer. I learned that people who use the SNHU Travel project don't care how the code works. They just want to be able to quickly and easily book flights and hotels. I find out what people need by asking them questions, reading what they say, and watching how they use the system.
In the future, I plan to use Agile practices such as sprint planning to organize tasks, daily stand-up meetings to discuss progress, and sprint reviews to get feedback from users. I will also use retrospectives to improve teamwork and solve problems earlier. These Agile processes help teams work more efficiently and improve software quality.

##What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
When you work on software, being a good team member means being clear when you talk, working with others, and helping the whole team succeed instead of just focusing on your own work. A good team member lets everyone know how things are going, asks questions if they don't understand something, and helps fix problems early. I learned during the project that problems get worse when people don't talk to each other and work alone.
A good team member also knows what each person is supposed to do. There are different jobs for developers, testers, Scrum Masters, and Product Owners, but they all need to work together to finish the project. It's important to listen to feedback and accept ideas from other people because software gets better when many people work on it. In general, a good team member is reliable, willing to work with others, and focused on helping the user rather than just finishing their own task.
